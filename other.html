Holistic Design Approach

The project considers not only the technical solution, but also cultural, environmental, and community factors. The design must respect Saibai community values and water-use practices. For example, separating rainwater and lagoon sources respects traditional rainwater harvesting while improving the safety of lagoon water. Controls and maintenance are kept simple and locally manageable to avoid long-term dependence on external specialists.

Construction and operation should minimise environmental impacts. UV disinfection is preferred over routine chlorination to deliver chemical-free treatment, reducing reliance on imported chemicals and the risk of disinfection by-products entering the environment. A clear waste-management protocol is required to collect, handle, and recycle non-local waste (e.g., spent filter cartridges and UV lamps), which may accumulate in significant volumes, to protect the island environment. Wherever possible, locally sourced materials are prioritised to minimise imported waste and packaging.

In addition, drinking-water turbidity should be maintained below 1 NTU to build trust, increase community confidence, and reduce energy-intensive household boiling. Reducing the need to boil water lowers household energy costs and benefits residents. The system uses a simple, low-maintenance layout that avoids complex machinery; training will be provided for local operators to support local employment, build pride and community ownership, and ensure straightforward upkeep. These considerations align with the Community Reaction Journey Map from Awareness to Full Acceptance.

Cultural Respect

Separate rainwater and lagoon sources to honour traditional rainwater harvesting while improving lagoon safety.

Community Empowerment

Keep controls and maintenance simple so the community can operate systems without long-term outside dependence.

Local Capacity Building

Provide operator training to create local jobs, build pride and ensure sustainable maintenance.

Environmental Sustainability

Chemical-Free Disinfection: UV disinfection is preferred over routine chlorination to avoid chemical handling and reduce risks of disinfection by-products entering the environment.
Waste Management Protocol: Collect, handle and recycle non-local wastes such as spent filter cartridges and UV lamps; where possible, prioritise locally sourced materials to minimise imported waste and packaging.

Community Health and Economic Benefits

  • Water Quality: Drinking-water turbidity maintained < 1 NTU to build trust and reduce household boiling.
  • Lower Costs: Less boiling → lower household energy expenses.
  • Ownership: Simple, low-maintenance layout + local training = easier upkeep and stronger community ownership.
