Targets We Commit To

Turbidity < 1 NTU
UV uptime ≥ 98%
Buffer stock 3–6 months

What We Recommend — Summary

Reliability

Keep a modest buffer of essentials

Filter cartridges, activated carbon, and UV lamps ensure smooth operation between supply runs.

  • Maintain 3–6 months of critical spares.
  • Agree alternates with suppliers in advance.
Maintenance

Simple, pictorial SOP & schedule

Clear steps make timely filter changes and fixes possible without specialist dependence.

  • One-page pictorial SOP + weekly/monthly checklist.
  • Quick troubleshooting flow (5 steps).
Transparency

Make water quality visible

Basic turbidity meter + small on-site display build trust and support the < 1 NTU target.

  • Show latest turbidity at the tank.
  • Use a simple green/amber UV status light.
Commissioning

Local fine-tuning, then scale

Verify UV dose & contact time; estimate filter life using local water data for confident OPEX planning.

  • Check UVT, lamp aging, sleeve fouling on-site.
  • Predict replacement intervals and costs.
These steps are standard optimisation practices that build on our design’s strengths and make rollout smoother.

Full Rationale

Show the exact text used in our recommendations

To lock in reliability in a remote setting, maintain a modest buffer of essential replacements—filter cartridges, activated carbon, and UV lamps. This is standard practice that keeps the system running smoothly between supply runs.

Adopt a simple, pictorial maintenance guide and schedule with clear steps for planning and quick troubleshooting. This helps ensure timely filter changes and routine upkeep without specialist dependence.

Enhance water-quality monitoring with a basic turbidity meter and a small on-site display of the latest reading. Making quality visible strengthens community trust and supports the < 1 NTU target.

As part of commissioning and local fine-tuning (not because of any design issue), verify UV dose and contact time under local conditions (e.g., UV transmittance, lamp aging, sleeve fouling) to confirm pathogen inactivation margins. Use local water data to estimate filter life so replacement intervals and costs are planned with confidence.
