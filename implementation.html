Overview

The implementation of the dual water system will be completed in three key stages over 18 months. Each phase integrates construction, community participation, and evaluation to ensure the system’s technical reliability and long-term sustainability. Collaboration between the Torres Strait Island Regional Council (TSIRC), Engineers Without Borders (EWB), and Saibai Island residents will ensure the project remains locally managed and culturally suitable (EWB Challenge, 2025).

Implementation Phases

Months 1 - 4

Phase 1: Construction and Community Training

Objective: Establish the foundation of the dual water system and develop local maintenance capacity.

Construction will begin with the installation of leaf guards and rooftop gutters to collect rainwater into sealed non-potable storage tanks. Locally sourced materials will be used to simplify maintenance and reduce long-term costs. At the same time, EWB and TSIRC will conduct training workshops to teach residents practical maintenance tasks such as cleaning leaf guards, inspecting pipe connections, and managing tanks.

For potable use, lagoon water will pass through a settling tank, multi-layer filtration, an ultrafiltration membrane, and a UV disinfection unit. Residents will be trained on how to maintain these systems safely and effectively (EWB Challenge, 2025).

Responsible Parties: EWB engineers, TSIRC technicians, local volunteers.

Months 5 - 10

Phase 2: System Introduction, Management, and Evaluation

Objective: Introduce the system to the community and implement management routines.

During this phase, demonstration sessions will help residents gain confidence in operating the system. Illustrated instruction manuals in the local language will be distributed to support daily use and troubleshooting. Monthly filter cleaning and annual inspections will be scheduled by the TSIRC environmental health team.

Routine turbidity testing will ensure that water consistently meets the <1 NTU safety target. Community feedback and annual sustainability reviews will be used to assess performance and identify improvement opportunities (EWB Challenge, 2025).

Responsible Parties: TSIRC environmental health team, community committee.

Months 11 - 18

Phase 3: Community Ownership and Ongoing Maintenance

Objective: Transition system ownership and maintenance to trained community members.

Operational responsibility will gradually be handed over to trained community teams, fostering long-term self-reliance. Residents will manage regular inspections and handle minor repairs using provided toolkits. TSIRC will continue to support the community by providing technical assistance for complex maintenance or upgrades (Torres Strait Island Regional Council, n.d.).

This phase aims to ensure that Saibai Island develops sustainable water management capacity, protecting both health and the environment for future generations.

Responsible Parties: Community management team, TSIRC support staff.

Expected Impact: By integrating construction, training, and community management, this plan ensures a resilient water system that maintains turbidity below 1 NTU. It enhances public health, strengthens local technical capacity, and empowers residents to sustainably manage their own water resources (EWB Challenge, 2025).
