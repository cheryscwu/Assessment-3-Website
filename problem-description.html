Problem Description

Saibai Island requires a reliable and sustainable water system that maintains turbidity below 1 NTU to ensure safe drinking water and protect community health (EWB Challenge, 2025).

Located in the north-western Torres Strait, Saibai Island faces ongoing water issues made worse by climate change. Rainwater tanks and lagoon catchments often show turbidity between 1 and 2 NTU, above the limit set by the Torres Strait Island Regional Council (n.d.).

Because freshwater access is limited, residents depend on transported water and diesel desalination. These options are costly, difficult to maintain, and environmentally unsustainable. A simple, low-cost, and energy-efficient system is needed to provide safe, consistent, and locally managed water for the island (EWB Challenge, 2025).

Design Criteria

The design criteria below guide the selection of a suitable water management system for Saibai Island, ensuring it meets community needs and TSIRC standards.

1 Water Quality and Safety

The system must maintain turbidity below 1 NTU to ensure clean, safe water while reducing contamination and disease risks.

Maintaining low turbidity improves water safety and public health (EWB Challenge, 2025).

2 Economic Feasibility and Simplicity

The design should be affordable, easy to maintain, and use durable materials suitable for Saibai’s remote environment.

Cost-effective systems support community self-management and long-term sustainability (Torres Strait Island Regional Council, n.d.).

3 Reliability and Resilience

The system must perform well under all weather conditions, providing year-round safe water for residents.

A resilient design ensures continuous water access and community wellbeing (EWB Challenge, 2025).
Goal: To develop a sustainable, affordable, and community-managed water system that provides safe and reliable access to clean water for Saibai Island residents.
